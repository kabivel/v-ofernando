## SLIDE 3 — Lesson 1

**Visual:** Big "01" left, title right, 3-line takeaway bottom.

```
01 · Discovery is not a checklist.
     It's a negotiation.

Half of "scope creep" is just discovery
you skipped. Shared mailboxes nobody
owns. Distribution lists tied to legacy
apps. Public folders with 12 years of
litigation evidence.

→ Spend 2x more time in discovery.
   You'll spend 5x less in remediation.
```

---

## SLIDE 4 — Lesson 2

```
02 · Identity comes first.
     Always. No exceptions.

If Entra ID isn't clean before you
migrate mailboxes, you're moving
broken accounts to a more expensive
platform.

Audit MFA coverage, conditional
access baselines, hybrid sync health,
and orphaned objects BEFORE
the first mailbox moves.

→ No clean identity, no migration.
```

---

## SLIDE 5 — Lesson 3

```
03 · Pilot users are not random.
     They are political.

The wrong 50 pilot users will kill
the rollout in week 2.

Pick: 1 executive sponsor, 2 vocal
skeptics, 5 power users from
different departments, and 1 person
from legal/compliance.

→ If the skeptics convert, the rest follow.
```

---

## SLIDE 6 — Lesson 4

```
04 · Communication beats tooling.

BitTitan, Quest On-Demand, native
cutover — all of them work.

What doesn't work: a 3-line email
on Friday saying "your mailbox
will move this weekend".

Build a comms cadence: T-30, T-14,
T-7, T-1, day-of, T+1, T+7.
Same template. Same channel.
Every wave.

→ Predictability is the feature users buy.
```

---

## SLIDE 7 — Lesson 5

```
05 · The project ends 90 days
     after go-live, not on go-live.

Day 0 = mailbox moved.
Day 90 = users actually changed behavior.

Plan for: license rebalancing, DLP
tuning, Conditional Access exceptions
cleanup, OneDrive known folder move,
adoption metrics review.

→ No post-migration plan = no ROI.
```
