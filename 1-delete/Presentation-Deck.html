Anticipated questions & prepared answers

Technical questions handled here. Commercial questions handed to the account team and the CSAM.

"Why is so much of our E5 unused? Did we buy the wrong SKU?"

"No — E5 is a very broad bundle, and almost every customer with E5 has a long list of unused capabilities. What is unusual is that your team took the time to map it. That puts you ahead of most organizations and lets us be deliberate about which capabilities to activate next."

"We were told Teams Phone would be first. Why change it?"

"Teams Phone is staying — it is being placed where it succeeds best. Doing it first means it competes for the organization's attention against capabilities that, if left off, carry real risk. Doing it last means it lands on a secure, well-adopted platform and becomes the visible win that closes the program."

"Can we do all three in parallel?"

"Technically yes, practically no. Parallel programs compete for the same change-management bandwidth in your organization. The single biggest reason transformation programs feel like they delivered no value is running too many fronts at once."

"How do you know what we are using vs not? You don't run our tenant."

"The charts you are seeing are built from the matrix your team produced — I am not inferring usage from the outside. The categorization is my read on where the unused features cluster, but the underlying data is yours."

"Is this just a way to sell us more services?"

"My role today is to give you an independent technical view. The commercial conversation is separate and lives with the account team. Even if you did this work entirely in-house, the sequence I am recommending — Secure, Adopt, Modernize — would still be the right one."

"Shouldn't AI be first since it's the strategic priority?"

"Copilot adoption without data governance is exactly how organizations end up with AI assistants surfacing sensitive content to the wrong audiences. Securing the data first is what makes the broader Copilot push safe to accelerate."

"What if we don't have the internal capacity for a security activation right now?"

"Then we right-size it. The activation does not have to be a big-bang program. The principle is the same: turn on what you already pay for, in priority order, at whatever pace the team can absorb."

"How long until we see value?"

"Early signals — visible policy enforcement, audit posture improvements — appear quickly once activation starts. Broad organizational value is felt during Step 2, when Copilot use scales. I would not put a number on it from this chair; that is a scoping conversation."

"What about cost? How much does this cost us?"

"Commercials are not my conversation — your account team will walk you through that. My contribution today is the technical sequence and the read of your own matrix."

"Can you give us a timeline / commit to dates?"

"Not from this conversation. Timelines come out of a scoping session where we look at your team's availability and your change calendar. Today's goal is alignment on the sequence — the account team and your CSAM will run the timing conversation with you."
