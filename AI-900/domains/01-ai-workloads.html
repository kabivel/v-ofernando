2. Responsible AI — 6 principles ⭐

Microsoft's framework. Memorize all 6 — at least 2–3 questions on the exam test these.

PrincipleOne-line definitionTrigger words in exam questions
FairnessSystem treats all people equitably"bias", "across demographic groups", "equal accuracy"
Reliability & SafetySystem performs reliably under expected and unexpected conditions"performs as expected", "fail-safe", "consistent results"
Privacy & SecuritySystem respects data privacy and protects against threats"PII", "consent", "encrypted", "data residency"
InclusivenessSystem empowers everyone, including people with disabilities"accessibility", "all abilities", "diverse users"
TransparencyPeople understand how the system makes decisions"explainability", "interpretable", "model card"
AccountabilityHumans remain responsible for AI outcomes"governance", "human oversight", "audit"

Reference: Responsible AI in Azure ML · Microsoft Responsible AI

3. Key considerations

  • Data quality drives model quality — biased / incomplete data = unfair model
  • Human oversight is required for high-impact decisions (hiring, lending, health)
  • Explainability trade-off: complex models (deep learning) are accurate but opaque; simpler models are interpretable
  • Content filters required for generative AI to prevent harmful output
  • Data residency: choose Azure region close to users for compliance (GDPR, LGPD)

4. Self-check

  1. An HR system rejects more candidates from one demographic. Which Responsible AI principle is violated?
  2. A medical AI explains why it flagged a tumor. Which principle does this fulfill?
  3. Voice assistant supports dysarthria-affected speech. Which principle?
  4. An ML model classifies emails as spam — which workload type?
  5. System extracts vendor name and total from a scanned invoice — workload?

Answers: 1. Fairness · 2. Transparency · 3. Inclusiveness · 4. Classification (under Machine Learning) · 5. Document intelligence
